Plug, Voltage & Time Zone
Power — Italy uses Type C, F and L plugs (2 round pins), 230V / 50Hz. Australian appliances (230-240V, 50Hz) generally work fine with just a plug adapter — a separate voltage converter usually isn't needed.
Time Zone — Italy is on CEST (UTC+2) during your trip. Australia doesn't observe daylight saving in May–June, so standard time zones apply: Italy is about 8 hours behind Sydney/Melbourne/Brisbane (AEST) and about 6 hours behind Perth (AWST) for the whole trip.

VAT Tax Refund Tips

As Australian (non-EU) residents, you can claim back part of the 22% VAT on eligible shopping. Italy simplified the system in 2024 and is rolling out an even faster digital process in mid-2026, so this should be quick by the time you travel.

Minimum spend: €70.01 in a single store, same day (much lower than the old €155 threshold).
Ask before you pay: request the Tax-Free Form from the cashier before the sale is finalised, and have your passport with you — Italy's digital system (OTELLO) links the purchase to your passport on the spot.
Keep items unused: don't wear or use anything until you're through customs — inspectors can ask to see the goods when validating.
Validate at your final EU exit point: for this trip that's Milan Malpensa Airport on Day 19, not each city you shop in. Allow extra time there for the customs/refund kiosk.
Actual refund: expect roughly 11–15% back, not the full 22% — refund operators deduct an administrative fee.
Rules can shift before 2027 — worth a quick check closer to departure. The VAT Tax Refund Guide link in Resources (Global Blue) has the official up-to-date process.
